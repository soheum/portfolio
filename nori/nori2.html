About
Nori Nuri is an interactive play-education platform for 3 to 5 year old children. Instead of being exposed to harmful contents through smart devices, children can interact with their favorite characters who are actually teachers giving out 30-minute long classes.
Challenge
Children in Korea are being exposed to harmful media through smart devices. Their brain development is hindered, and blocking services are inefficient. Our solution tackles both problems, especially regarding smart devices now have become an irresistable childcare tool.
We tested in total 2 children with beta versions of Nori Nuri. Results varied because the second user test familiarized the children with Nori by showing pictures of Nori before the test date. Test results vary as follows:
This result illustrates how children showed more concentration and interest in characters that they know beforehand.

Therefore, we decided to add a beginner’s curriculum that children can complete with their parents. This class will deal about how to get used to Nori and Nuri, along with introducing themselves to Nori and Nuri.
Nori Nuri was the first project that I involved in starting from the very basics until the end, where we actually had a beta version prototype. It was a challenge for me to find painpoints for users and come up with a creative solution that will not only solve these painpoints, but also create social value and joy for users.

It was also a project where I discovered the significance of interaction within new technology in the 21st century. Interaction can redefine new relationships between user and product (or service) and further allow users to utilize technology in a method that can benefit us. Nori Nuri was not only a project that solved user’s needs successfully, but also a service that best describes my goals in design.

RESEARCH : INTERVIEW
From our user researach, we found out how blocking programs are of no use, especially since smartphone usage is already prevalent enough among children. Instead of making another blocking program that will also have little effect, we had to come up with a new solution that will change the paradigm of children’s smartphone usage.
