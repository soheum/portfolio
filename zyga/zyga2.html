About
Zygä is a wheel registry and sharing platform, aimed to increase the use of already existing resources such as bicycles, encouraging their repair. Users can borrow other wheels, lend their wheels to others, or even purchase partial materials for their wheel repair.
Challenge
Zygä had to be launched in a short time schedule, especially the design had to be redone to change from a mobile version to web version. I had to communicate with our startup’s developer, in matching the interface with Bootstrap designs. After its launch, I participated in fixing the platform’s bugs or problems, which demanded a very quick and rapid schedule.

Starting from meeting this Finnish startup in Slush, working remotely from Finland to Korea, and finally going to Helsinki during the summer to work required extensive amount of communication skills and challenge. When working remotely, I had to express my thoughts clearly as possible since there are limitations that exist when communicating online. Furthermore, inviting our startup to Korea for a lecture and myself going to Helsinki was always a chance to challenge each other.

The startup ecosystem also challenged me to work on short deadlines, especially when we had to change our service’s release from a mobile application to a website. With having a 2 week deadline, design outcomes had to change from a mobile version to web version, and I cooperated with our team’s developer in utilizing Bootstrap libraries.
